# 🍫 Chocolate Sales Analysis Project

Welcome to my **Chocolate Sales Analysis Project** — an ongoing end-to-end data analytics project where I analyze business performance using SQL, data modeling, and business intelligence thinking.

The goal of this project is not simply to practice SQL syntax, but to develop the ability to solve **real-world executive-level business problems** using data.

Instead of working on random SQL exercises, I structured this project to simulate how analysts work in actual companies: receiving business questions from leadership, understanding the data, designing the logic needed to answer the question, writing SQL queries, and finally interpreting the business story behind the results.

---

## Project Overview

This project started with a raw chocolate sales dataset sourced from Kaggle.

Before beginning any analysis, I first focused on building the data foundation by:

- Cleaning and understanding the raw dataset  
- Designing a proper **Star Schema** for analytical querying  
- Creating a structured **PostgreSQL Data Warehouse**  
- Building fact and dimension tables for efficient business analysis  

The idea was simple:

**No meaningful analysis can happen without properly structured data.**

## Current Work Completed

So far, I have been working on solving executive-level business questions designed to simulate real business decision-making.

### 1. Loyalty Program Impact Analysis

Business question:

> For each store type, analyze the impact of the loyalty program by showing total orders placed, total profit generated, percentage of profit contributed by registered loyalty members, focusing only on the top-performing product category by revenue.

Key concepts applied:

- Common Table Expressions (CTEs)  
- Multi-table JOINs  
- GROUP BY for business segmentation  
- CASE statements for conditional aggregation  
- COUNT(DISTINCT) to avoid double counting  
- Business interpretation of loyalty contribution to profitability  

Main insight:

Approximately **50% of total profit across all sales channels is consistently generated by loyalty members**, suggesting the loyalty program is a major and stable driver of profitability across the business.

---

### 2. Country Financial Health Analysis (2024 Performance)

Business question:

> For each country, analyze overall financial health for the year 2024 by showing total quantity sold, total revenue generated, average profit margin percentage, focusing only on countries generating more than $2 million in revenue.

Key concepts applied:

- Time-based filtering using dates  
- Aggregations with SUM()  
- Profit margin calculations  
- HAVING clause for post-aggregation filtering  
- ORDER BY for ranking top-performing markets  
- Business interpretation of revenue and profitability patterns  

Main insight:

**Australia emerged as the strongest performing market with the highest revenue and quantity sold, while profit margins remained highly consistent across countries, suggesting standardized pricing strategy and operational consistency globally.**

---

### 3. Discount Impact Analysis (2024 Performance)

Business question :

>We’ve been spending a lot on discounts and promotions lately, and I need to understand whether those discounts are actually helping the business or simply reducing profitability.I want you to analyze product category performance for the year 2024.For each product category, show me :total number of orders placed, total revenue generated, average discount percentage given, average profit margin percentage

Key Concepts applied: 

- JOINs  
- GROUP BY  
- COUNT(DISTINCT)  
- SUM() and AVG() Aggregations  
- Profit Margin Calculation  
- Date-Based Filtering  
- ORDER BY Ranking  
- Business Performance Analysis  

Main insight:

**Our discounts are not hurting product profitability, but there is little evidence that they are driving stronger business performance either. Product revenue differences are being driven by product demand, not discount strategy.**
